# Benchmark rubric\n\nUse this rubric to compare `pm-workbench` against generic AI outputs.\n\n## Scoring scale\n\nScore each criterion from **0 to 3**:\n- **0 — Missed badly**\n- **1 — Weak / partial**\n- **2 — Solid**\n- **3 — Strong / clearly better than average generic AI**\n\nSuggested total range if using all 7 criteria:\n- **0-7** -> weak PM support\n- **8-13** -> mixed / unreliable\n- **14-17** -> strong practical PM support\n- **18-21** -> excellent and clearly differentiated\n\n---\n\n## 1. Upstream problem framing\n\n### What to judge\nDid the answer solve the right PM problem first?\n\n### Strong signal\n- clarifies before evaluating when needed\n- identifies embedded solution assumptions\n- reframes vague asks into a decision-ready problem\n\n### Weak signal\n- jumps straight into solutions\n- accepts the prompt framing uncritically\n\n---\n\n## 2. Follow-up question quality\n\n### What to judge\nIf the model asked questions, were they selective and decision-relevant?\n\n### Strong signal\n- asks only the 1-3 questions that would materially change the answer\n- avoids questionnaire behavior\n- skips unnecessary questioning when context is already sufficient\n\n### Weak signal\n- asks too many generic context questions\n- asks none when the recommendation obviously depends on missing premises\n\n---\n\n## 3. Recommendation quality\n\n### What to judge\nDid the answer make a usable call?\n\n### Strong signal\n- gives a clear recommendation, decision, or next move\n- labels confidence and assumptions honestly when needed\n- avoids “it depends” as the final state\n\n### Weak signal\n- only lists considerations\n- stays symmetrical and non-committal\n\n---\n\n## 4. Trade-off and non-decision clarity\n\n### What to judge\nDid the answer show what is gained, lost, delayed, or displaced?\n\n### Strong signal\n- explains opportunity cost\n- says what should wait or what is below the line\n- makes trade-offs legible for stakeholder discussion\n\n### Weak signal\n- only talks about upside\n- avoids explicit trade-offs\n\n---\n\n## 5. Artifact reuse quality\n\n### What to judge\nWould a PM or product leader reuse this output with light editing?\n\n### Strong signal\n- naturally fits a memo, brief, scorecard, roadmap, or summary shape\n- is structured for a real meeting, review, or decision flow\n- feels operationally useful, not just polished\n\n### Weak signal\n- sounds smart but is hard to reuse\n- lacks structure or a reusable artifact shape\n\n---\n\n## 6. Product-leader relevance\n\n### What to judge\nDoes the answer help with leadership-grade product work, not just IC analysis?\n\n### Strong signal\n- surfaces business consequence, resourcing, sequencing, and stakeholder asks\n- reflects portfolio or company-level constraints when relevant\n- makes upward communication easier\n\n### Weak signal\n- stays at isolated feature level when the real issue is portfolio or leadership alignment\n- ignores resource implications\n\n---\n\n## 7. Honesty about uncertainty\n\n### What to judge\nDid the answer distinguish facts, assumptions, and missing evidence?\n\n### Strong signal\n- labels missing premises clearly\n- says what could change the recommendation\n- avoids fake certainty\n\n### Weak signal\n- overstates confidence\n- hides gaps behind polished language\n\n---\n\n## Practical comparison rule\n\nA generic AI answer can still score well on writing style.\nIgnore that as a primary criterion.\n\nThis rubric is designed to reward:\n- PM judgment\n- decisiveness\n- artifact usefulness\n- leader-grade framing\n- honest handling of uncertainty\n\nThose are the dimensions where `pm-workbench` should win.\n
